Page 4: Refrigerator and Freezer Maintenance

Your refrigerator runs 24/7/365—it's one of the biggest energy users in your home. Simple maintenance keeps it running efficiently and can extend its life by years.

Optimal Temperature Settings

Compartment Ideal Temperature
Refrigerator 35-38°F (2-3°C)
Freezer 0°F (-18°C)
Check with a Thermometer: The dial settings (1-9 or cold/colder) aren't precise. A refrigerator thermometer ($5-10) tells you the actual temperature.

Monthly Maintenance

Clean the Condenser Coils

Dusty coils make your fridge work harder and use more energy.

  1. Locate coils (back of fridge or behind a kick plate at the bottom)
  2. Unplug the refrigerator (or turn off at breaker)
  3. Use a vacuum with brush attachment or a coil cleaning brush
  4. Gently remove dust and debris
  5. Plug back in

Do this every 6-12 months (more often with pets)

Clean Door Gaskets

The rubber seals around the door keep cold air in. Dirty or damaged gaskets waste energy.

  • Wipe with warm, soapy water monthly
  • Check for cracks, tears, or mold
  • Test the seal: close the door on a dollar bill—it should resist when you pull

Efficiency Tips

  • Don't overfill: Air needs to circulate. Leave some space between items.
  • Don't underfill the freezer: A full freezer is more efficient (frozen items help keep each other cold).
  • Let hot food cool: Before putting in fridge, let hot leftovers cool to room temp (within 2 hours for food safety).
  • Cover liquids: Uncovered liquids release moisture, making the compressor work harder.
  • Check the door: Make sure it closes completely and seals properly.
Stop and Call a Pro If...
  • The fridge runs constantly without cycling off
  • You hear loud clicking, buzzing, or humming
  • Water is pooling inside or under the fridge
  • Food is freezing in the refrigerator section
  • The freezer isn't maintaining 0°F

Ice Maker and Water Dispenser

  • Change the water filter: Every 6 months (or per manufacturer recommendation)
  • Clean the ice bin: Wash with warm soapy water every few months
  • If ice tastes bad: Old ice absorbs odors—dump it and make fresh

Action Items

  1. Check your fridge and freezer temperatures
  2. Inspect and clean the door gaskets
  3. Locate the condenser coils for future cleaning
  4. Note when the water filter was last changed (if applicable)
