Page 3: Thermostat Efficiency

Your thermostat is the brain of your HVAC system. Using it wisely can save 10-15% on heating and cooling costs—that's potentially hundreds of dollars per year!

Thermostat Types

Manual (Non-Programmable)

Simple dial or digital display. You set the temperature; it stays there until you change it.

Best for: Consistent schedules or rental properties

Programmable

Allows you to set different temperatures for different times of day. "Set it and forget it" savings.

Best for: Regular schedules (work 9-5, sleep same time)

Smart Thermostat

Learns your habits, can be controlled via smartphone, and optimizes for efficiency. Brands: Nest, Ecobee, Honeywell Home.

Best for: Tech-savvy users, irregular schedules, maximum savings

Recommended Temperature Settings

Situation Winter (Heating) Summer (Cooling)
Home and awake 68°F (20°C) 78°F (26°C)
Sleeping 65°F (18°C) 78-80°F (26-27°C)
Away from home 60-62°F (16-17°C) 85°F (29°C)
The 1-Degree Rule: Each degree you lower your heat (winter) or raise your AC (summer) saves about 1-3% on your energy bill. Small adjustments add up!

Energy-Saving Strategies

Setback Strategy

Lower the heat or raise the AC when you're away or sleeping. Don't turn it OFF completely—just set it back.

  • Winter: Set to 62°F when away, 65°F when sleeping
  • Summer: Set to 85°F when away (prevents humidity issues)

Avoid the "Blast" Myth

Setting your thermostat to 85°F won't heat your home faster than setting it to 70°F. It just makes the system run longer and overshoot.

Fan Settings

  • AUTO: Fan runs only when heating/cooling (recommended for most)
  • ON: Fan runs continuously (uses more energy, but circulates air constantly)
Ceiling Fan Trick: In summer, ceiling fans make rooms feel 4°F cooler. Run fans and raise the AC by 4° for same comfort at lower cost. (Remember: fans cool people, not rooms—turn them off when you leave!)

Troubleshooting

Thermostat Not Responding

  1. Check if it needs batteries (most programmable thermostats use AA or AAA)
  2. Check the HVAC breaker
  3. Make sure the system switch is in HEAT, COOL, or AUTO—not OFF

Temperature Doesn't Match Setting

  • Give the system time—it takes a while to change temperature
  • Check that vents are open and unblocked
  • Ensure the thermostat isn't in direct sunlight or near a heat source
  • Dirty filter can prevent proper heating/cooling

Action Items

  1. Identify your thermostat type
  2. Check if your thermostat needs batteries
  3. If programmable, set up a basic schedule
  4. Try adjusting your temperature 2° and see if you notice
