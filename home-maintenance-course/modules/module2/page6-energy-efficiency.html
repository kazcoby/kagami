Page 6: Energy Efficiency Quick Wins

You don't need to spend thousands on solar panels or new appliances to save money on energy. These low-cost and no-cost changes can cut your bills by 10-25%.

No-Cost Changes (Do Today!)

Adjust Thermostat

Lower heat 2° in winter, raise AC 2° in summer. Savings: 3-5% on heating/cooling.

Use Natural Light

Open blinds during the day instead of turning on lights.

Unplug "Vampires"

Phone chargers, game consoles, and TVs use power even when off. Unplug or use power strips.

Wash Cold

Wash clothes in cold water. 90% of washing machine energy goes to heating water.

Full Loads Only

Run dishwasher and laundry only when full. Same energy for half-load.

Skip the Heat Dry

Let dishes air dry. Open dishwasher after rinse cycle and let them dry naturally.

Water Heating (Your 2nd Biggest Energy User)

Quick Water Heater Wins

  • Lower the temperature: Set to 120°F (many come set to 140°F). Saves energy and reduces scalding risk.
  • Insulate hot water pipes: Foam insulation from hardware store. Focus on first 3 feet from water heater.
  • Fix drips: A hot water drip wastes 1,660 gallons/year.
  • Take shorter showers: Each minute less = 2.5 gallons saved × energy to heat it.

Seasonal Efficiency Tips

Summer

  • Close blinds on south and west windows during peak sun
  • Use ceiling fans (counterclockwise direction pushes air down)
  • Avoid using oven—grill outside or use microwave/instant pot
  • Run heat-producing appliances (dryer, dishwasher) at night

Winter

  • Open south-facing blinds during the day for solar heat
  • Close blinds at night to insulate windows
  • Reverse ceiling fan direction (clockwise on low pushes warm air down)
  • Use draft stoppers at exterior doors
  • Keep garage door closed (if attached to house)
Check for Air Leaks: On a windy day, hold a lit incense stick near windows, doors, and electrical outlets on exterior walls. Smoke movement reveals leaks. Seal with caulk or weatherstripping.

Understanding Your Energy Bill

  • kWh (kilowatt-hours): The unit of electricity you're billed for
  • Therms: The unit for natural gas
  • Compare month-to-month: Look at usage, not just cost (rates change)
  • Year-over-year: Compare same month last year for best comparison

Action Items

  1. Check your water heater temperature—lower to 120°F if higher
  2. Identify 3 "vampire" devices to unplug or put on a power strip
  3. Count your incandescent bulbs and consider LED replacements
  4. Look at your last 3 utility bills—spot any trends?
