Page 5: Washer, Dryer, and Dishwasher Care

Washing Machine

Monthly Maintenance

  • Clean the dispenser: Remove and rinse detergent/fabric softener dispensers
  • Wipe the door seal (front-loaders): Mold loves hiding in the rubber gasket
  • Run a cleaning cycle: Use washing machine cleaner or 2 cups white vinegar on hot
  • Leave the door open: After washing, leave it ajar to prevent mildew

Prevent Problems

  • Don't overload—clothes need room to move
  • Use HE detergent for HE machines (less suds)
  • Check pockets for coins, pens, tissues
  • Don't leave wet clothes sitting—transfer promptly
Hose Check: Inspect washer supply hoses yearly. Rubber hoses should be replaced every 5 years. Consider upgrading to braided stainless steel hoses—they're less likely to burst.

Dryer

FIRE HAZARD: Lint buildup causes 15,000+ home fires yearly!

Clean the lint trap after EVERY load. This is not optional.

Essential Dryer Maintenance

After Every Load

  • Clean the lint trap/screen

Monthly

  • Wash the lint screen with soap and water (dryer sheet residue can clog it)
  • Vacuum around and under the dryer
  • Check the vent hood outside—make sure it opens when dryer runs

Yearly

  • Clean the entire dryer vent duct (consider professional cleaning)
  • Pull dryer away from wall and vacuum behind
  • Check the vent connection for damage or disconnection
Signs Your Dryer Vent is Clogged:
  • Clothes take longer than one cycle to dry
  • Clothes are very hot when cycle ends
  • The dryer itself is hot to touch
  • Burning smell when running
  • Vent hood flap doesn't open during operation

If you notice these signs, stop using the dryer and clean or have the vent professionally cleaned.

Dishwasher

Monthly Maintenance

  • Clean the filter: Bottom of the dishwasher—twist and remove, rinse under water
  • Check spray arms: Clear any debris from the holes
  • Wipe door edges: Grime builds up where the door meets the tub
  • Run a cleaning cycle: Empty dishwasher + 1 cup vinegar in a bowl on top rack, hot cycle

Better Results

  • Scrape, don't rinse—modern dishwashers need some food to work against
  • Don't block the spray arms with large items
  • Load properly: face soiled surfaces toward the center/spray
  • Use rinse aid for spot-free dishes
Stop and Call a Pro If...
  • Water doesn't drain after the cycle
  • Dishes come out with a white film (may need water softener adjustment)
  • You smell burning or see sparks
  • Water leaks from the door or underneath

Action Items

  1. Clean your dryer lint trap right now (if you haven't today)
  2. Check when you last cleaned your washer—run a cleaning cycle if needed
  3. Locate your dishwasher filter and check its condition
  4. Inspect washer hoses for bulges, cracks, or age
