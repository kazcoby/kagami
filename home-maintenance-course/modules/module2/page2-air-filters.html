Page 2: Air Filters

Changing your HVAC filter is the single most important maintenance task you can do. A clean filter improves air quality, reduces energy costs, and protects your expensive HVAC equipment.

The #1 Cause of HVAC Breakdowns? Dirty filters. When airflow is restricted, the system works harder, overheats, and components fail prematurely. A $5-20 filter can prevent a $500+ repair!

Finding Your Filter

Air filters are typically located in one of these places:

  • Return air vent: A large vent on a wall or ceiling (most common in apartments)
  • Furnace/air handler: Inside the blower compartment (common in houses with basement furnaces)
  • Slot near the HVAC unit: A dedicated filter slot before air enters the system

Filter Sizes

Filters come in standard sizes listed as length × width × depth in inches. Common sizes include:

  • 16×20×1
  • 16×25×1
  • 20×20×1
  • 20×25×1
  • 20×25×4 (thicker, longer-lasting)
How to Find Your Size: Look at your current filter—the size is printed on the frame. If it's worn off, measure the filter (or the slot) with a measuring tape.

Filter Types and MERV Ratings

MERV Rating Type Best For Change Frequency
1-4 Fiberglass (flat, blue) Basic dust, protects equipment only Every 30 days
5-8 Pleated (white, folded) Dust, pollen, mold spores Every 60-90 days
9-12 High-efficiency pleated Allergies, better air quality Every 60-90 days
13-16 Hospital-grade Severe allergies, smoke May restrict airflow—check with HVAC pro
Higher Isn't Always Better! Very high MERV ratings (13+) can restrict airflow too much for some residential systems, causing damage. For most homes, MERV 8-11 is the sweet spot—good filtration without stressing your system.

How to Change Your Filter

1Turn off the system. Switch the thermostat to OFF or turn off the furnace switch. This prevents the blower from running while you work.

2Locate the filter. Open the return vent cover (usually held by clips or screws) or the furnace access panel.

3Note the airflow direction. Look for an arrow on the filter frame—this shows which way air should flow (toward the blower/furnace).

4Remove the old filter. Slide it out carefully. If it's very dirty, put it in a garbage bag to avoid spreading dust.

5Insert the new filter. Make sure the arrow points in the correct direction (toward the furnace/away from the room).

6Close and restart. Replace the cover and turn the system back on.

7Write the date on the filter. Use a marker to note when you installed it.

Change Frequency Guide

  • 1" standard filter: Every 1-3 months
  • 4" thick filter: Every 6-12 months
  • With pets: Change more frequently (pet dander clogs filters faster)
  • During heavy use: Check monthly during summer AC season
  • Construction/renovation: Check weekly (drywall dust destroys filters)
Pro Tip: Buy filters in bulk (4-12 at a time) so you always have one ready. Set a phone reminder for your change schedule!

Action Items

  1. Locate your air filter
  2. Check and note the size
  3. Examine its condition—is it gray/clogged or still relatively clean?
  4. Buy replacement filters in your size
  5. Set a calendar reminder to check it monthly
