Module 4: Simple Repairs + Maintenance System

Estimated time: 2-3 hours

Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

  • Patch small holes in drywall
  • Re-caulk around tubs, sinks, and windows
  • Tighten or adjust cabinet hardware
  • Silence squeaky doors and hinges
  • Unclog and maintain garbage disposals
  • Establish a seasonal home maintenance calendar
  • Know when repairs are DIY vs. requiring a professional
  • Communicate effectively with landlords and contractors

Why This Matters

Small repairs left unaddressed become big problems. A tiny crack in caulk lets water into walls. A loose hinge leads to a damaged door frame. A squeaky floor becomes a safety hazard. This module teaches you the quick fixes that prevent expensive repairs—and the system to stay on top of maintenance before problems start.

The Compound Effect of Maintenance:

Spending 15 minutes a month on preventive maintenance can save thousands in emergency repairs. Regular upkeep also protects your security deposit (renters) and home value (owners).

What We WON'T Cover:
  • Drywall repairs larger than 6 inches
  • Any structural repairs
  • Electrical work beyond changing bulbs/batteries
  • Plumbing beyond what we covered in Module 3
  • Roofing or exterior repairs requiring ladders over 6 feet

These require licensed professionals!

Materials You May Need

You won't need all of these right away, but here's what the module covers:

  • Basic tool kit (screwdrivers, pliers, hammer, tape measure)
  • Spackle or drywall compound (small container)
  • Putty knife
  • Sandpaper (fine grit, 120-150)
  • Caulk and caulk gun
  • WD-40 or silicone lubricant
  • Touch-up paint (matching your walls)
Renter Note: Before making any repairs, check your lease! Some landlords prefer you report issues rather than fix them yourself. Others appreciate (and may reimburse) minor repairs. When in doubt, ask first and get permission in writing.
