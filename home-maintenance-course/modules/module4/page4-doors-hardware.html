Page 4: Doors, Hinges, and Hardware Fixes

Squeaky hinges, sticking doors, and loose cabinet handles are annoying—but they're also some of the easiest fixes in home maintenance. Most take under 10 minutes and require minimal tools.

Problem: Squeaky Door Hinges

Cause: Friction between metal parts, often from dust or dried lubricant.

Time to fix: 2-5 minutes

Supplies: WD-40, silicone spray, or even cooking spray/petroleum jelly in a pinch

Quick Fix

  1. Open the door partially
  2. Spray lubricant on the hinge pins (the vertical rods) and the hinge joints
  3. Open and close the door several times to work in the lubricant
  4. Wipe away excess

Thorough Fix (for persistent squeaks)

  1. Support the door (use a book underneath to hold weight)
  2. Use a hammer and screwdriver or nail to tap the hinge pin upward
  3. Remove the pin completely
  4. Clean off old gunk with a rag
  5. Coat the pin with lubricant or petroleum jelly
  6. Reinsert the pin, tapping back into place
  7. Repeat for remaining hinges if needed
Pro Tip: Silicone spray lasts longer than WD-40 and doesn't attract dust. For best results, remove the pin and apply lubricant directly.

Problem: Door Won't Latch

Cause: Strike plate misalignment (the door has shifted or settled).

Time to fix: 5-15 minutes

Diagnose the Problem

  1. Close the door slowly and watch where the latch meets the strike plate
  2. Look for marks on the strike plate showing where the latch is hitting
  3. If the latch is missing the hole, the plate needs to move

Fix 1: Adjust the Strike Plate

  1. Remove the strike plate screws
  2. Move the plate slightly in the direction needed
  3. Mark new screw positions with pencil
  4. Fill old holes with toothpicks and wood glue (let dry)
  5. Drill pilot holes in new positions
  6. Reinstall strike plate

Fix 2: Enlarge the Strike Plate Opening

If the latch is just barely missing:

  1. Remove the strike plate
  2. Use a metal file to enlarge the opening slightly
  3. Reinstall and test

Problem: Door Sticks or Drags

Cause: Humidity (wood swelling), loose hinges, or settling.

Check Hinges First

  1. Tighten all hinge screws (both on door and frame)
  2. If screws spin freely, the holes are stripped (see fix below)
  3. Often this alone fixes the sticking

Fix Stripped Screw Holes

  1. Remove the screw
  2. Insert wooden toothpicks into the hole (2-3 usually)
  3. Break off flush with surface
  4. Optionally add a drop of wood glue
  5. Reinstall screw—it will now grip the wood
  6. For more holding power, replace with longer screws (2-3")

If Hinges Are Tight But Door Still Sticks

  1. Identify where it's sticking (look for rub marks)
  2. If it's humidity-related, a dehumidifier may help
  3. For minor sticking, rub candle wax or bar soap on the sticking edge
  4. For persistent sticking, you may need to sand or plane the edge (more advanced)

Problem: Loose Cabinet Handles/Knobs

Cause: Screws loosen from regular use.

Time to fix: 1-2 minutes per handle

For Knobs (Single Screw)

  1. Hold the knob steady
  2. Tighten the screw from inside the cabinet/drawer
  3. Don't over-tighten (can crack the cabinet face)

For Handles/Pulls (Two Screws)

  1. Tighten both screws evenly from inside
  2. If they keep loosening, add a drop of thread-lock (blue Loctite)
  3. Or use slightly longer screws

Problem: Drawer Slides Poorly

Cause: Dirty slides, worn tracks, or misalignment.

Fix

  1. Remove the drawer completely
  2. Clean the slides/tracks with a rag
  3. Apply silicone spray or rub with candle wax
  4. Check for bent tracks or loose screws
  5. Reinstall drawer and test

Problem: Bifold/Closet Door Off Track

Cause: Pivot pins or rollers come out of track, especially with rough use.

Fix

  1. Open the door to access the top track
  2. Lift the door and guide the top roller back into track
  3. Check bottom pivot—is it seated in the bracket?
  4. Adjust the bottom pivot height if door drags (there's usually a screw)
  5. Tighten track mounting screws if loose

Problem: Door Slams Too Hard

Solutions:

  • Adhesive door bumpers: Stick-on rubber or felt bumps ($3-5 pack)
  • Hinge pin door closer: Installs on hinge, controls closing speed ($10-15)
  • Hydraulic door closer: Mounted on door/frame, best for exterior or heavy doors ($30+)

Tools for Door/Hardware Fixes

  • Phillips and flathead screwdrivers
  • Hammer (for hinge pins)
  • WD-40 or silicone spray
  • Toothpicks and wood glue (for stripped holes)
  • Metal file (for strike plates)
  • Candle wax or bar soap (lubricant)
Stop and Call a Pro If...
  • Door frame is visibly warped or damaged
  • Door won't close at all and adjustments don't help
  • There are large gaps around the door (energy/security issue)
  • Exterior door has security or weather-sealing issues
  • You need to plane or cut a door (requires specialized tools)
  • Problem is with the door lock mechanism itself

Renter vs. Homeowner

Renters:

  • Squeaky hinges and loose handles: Generally fine to fix yourself
  • Sticking doors: Report to landlord, especially if it affects security
  • Don't modify door hardware without permission

Homeowners:

  • All of these are DIY-appropriate
  • Addressing issues early prevents damage to frames and floors
  • Consider upgrading to soft-close hinges for kitchen cabinets

Action Items

  1. Walk through your home and note any squeaky hinges
  2. Check all cabinet handles for looseness
  3. Test doors for proper latching
  4. Make a list of any issues to address
