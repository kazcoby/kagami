Page 5: Your Maintenance Calendar System

The best home maintenance is preventive. Small tasks done regularly prevent big, expensive problems. This page gives you a complete framework for staying on top of maintenance without feeling overwhelmed.

The 15-Minute Principle:

Most monthly maintenance tasks take 15 minutes or less. Scheduling just one Sunday afternoon per month keeps your home running smoothly year-round.

Monthly Tasks (Every Month)

Quick Monthly Checklist

  • HVAC filter: Check, replace if dirty (or per manufacturer schedule)
  • Test smoke/CO detectors: Press test button
  • Test GFCIs: Press test/reset buttons
  • Check under sinks: Look for leaks, moisture
  • Run unused drains: Prevents P-trap from drying out
  • Clean garbage disposal: Ice cubes + salt, then lemon
  • Check fire extinguisher: Gauge in green zone?

Estimated time: 15-20 minutes

Seasonal Deep Dives

Spring (March-May)

Theme: Recover from winter, prepare for summer

  • Replace smoke/CO detector batteries (even if working)
  • Clean refrigerator coils
  • Test A/C before hot weather hits
  • Check window and door screens for damage
  • Inspect caulking around tubs, sinks, windows
  • Clean range hood filter
  • Check outdoor faucets for winter damage (if applicable)
  • Clean dryer vent thoroughly (or schedule pro cleaning)
  • Inspect roof for winter damage (from ground level)
  • Check weather stripping on doors

Summer (June-August)

Theme: A/C efficiency, pest prevention

  • Clean A/C outdoor unit (remove debris, trim plants)
  • Check A/C drain line (clear if clogged)
  • Inspect and clean ceiling fans
  • Check for gaps where pests could enter
  • Test garage door safety reverse
  • Flush water heater (or schedule professional)
  • Deep clean bathroom exhaust fans
  • Inspect deck/patio for needed repairs (homeowners)
  • Check attic for proper ventilation (homeowners)

Fall (September-November)

Theme: Prepare for winter, heating system ready

  • Replace smoke/CO detector batteries (second time)
  • Schedule furnace/heating inspection (before cold hits)
  • Replace furnace filter (fresh for heating season)
  • Test heating system before you need it
  • Reverse ceiling fan direction (clockwise for winter)
  • Check weather stripping and door sweeps
  • Drain and store garden hoses (cold climates)
  • Shut off exterior faucets (cold climates)
  • Clean gutters (homeowners)
  • Check insulation around pipes in unheated areas
  • Stock winter emergency supplies

Winter (December-February)

Theme: Interior focus, energy efficiency

  • Test carbon monoxide detectors (heating season critical)
  • Check for drafts around windows and doors
  • Monitor humidity levels (winter air is dry)
  • Know how to prevent frozen pipes (if applicable)
  • Inspect fire extinguisher
  • Clean refrigerator coils (second time)
  • Test water heater pressure relief valve
  • Check bathroom/kitchen caulk
  • Plan spring projects during indoor time

Setting Up Your System

Option 1: Digital Calendar

  • Create recurring events in Google Calendar, Outlook, or Apple Calendar
  • Set reminders 1 day before and day-of
  • Include task list in the event description
  • Color-code maintenance vs. other events

Option 2: Home Maintenance App

  • HomeZada: Tracks tasks, appliances, documents, expenses
  • Centriq: Stores manuals, sends maintenance reminders
  • BrightNest: Simple tips and task scheduling

Option 3: Paper System

  • Print a yearly maintenance calendar (see templates)
  • Keep in home binder or on refrigerator
  • Check off tasks as completed
  • Add to your regular calendar or planner
Whatever System You Choose:

The best system is one you'll actually use. A simple paper checklist beats a complex app you ignore. Start simple and add complexity only if needed.

Tracking and Documentation

Keep records of:

  • When tasks were completed — Helps establish maintenance patterns
  • Professional service dates — For warranty and resale
  • Receipts — Tax deductions (homeowners), reimbursement (renters)
  • Appliance ages and model numbers — Plan for replacements
  • Paint colors and where they're used — For touch-ups
Don't Skip These

These maintenance tasks, if skipped, lead to the most expensive repairs:

  • HVAC filter changes (system failure)
  • Dryer vent cleaning (fire hazard)
  • Leak inspection (water damage, mold)
  • Caulk maintenance (water intrusion)
  • Smoke/CO detector testing (life safety)

Action Items

  1. Choose your maintenance tracking system (digital, app, or paper)
  2. Download or create your seasonal maintenance calendar
  3. Schedule your first "Monthly Maintenance Day"
  4. Identify any annual services you need to schedule
  5. Create a home binder or folder for receipts and records
