Page 4: GFCI Outlets - Testing and Resetting

GFCI outlets are one of the most important safety devices in your home. Understanding how they work and how to reset them is a fundamental skill every renter and homeowner should have.

What is a GFCI?

GFCI = Ground Fault Circuit Interrupter

A GFCI monitors electricity flowing through a circuit. If it detects electricity going somewhere it shouldn't (like through water or a person), it cuts power in 1/40th of a second—fast enough to prevent serious shock.

Identifying GFCI Outlets

What They Look Like

GFCI outlets have two buttons between the plug slots:

  • TEST button: Usually black or darker colored
  • RESET button: Usually red (may pop out when tripped)

Some also have a small indicator light.

Where You'll Find Them

Code requires GFCIs in areas near water:

  • Bathrooms (all outlets)
  • Kitchens (countertop outlets near sink)
  • Garages
  • Outdoors
  • Basements and crawl spaces
  • Laundry areas
  • Near pools, hot tubs, or fountains
One GFCI Can Protect Many Outlets!

A single GFCI outlet can protect other "downstream" outlets on the same circuit. If a regular outlet in your bathroom suddenly has no power, check for a tripped GFCI elsewhere—often in the same bathroom or even another bathroom.

Why GFCIs Trip

Common causes:

  • Moisture: Water in the outlet or on a connected device
  • Worn appliances: Old hair dryers, curling irons, or power tools
  • Overloaded circuits: Too many devices on one circuit
  • Worn GFCI: They don't last forever (10-15 year lifespan)
  • Ground faults: Damaged wiring or insulation

How to Reset a Tripped GFCI

1Identify the problem — What was plugged in when it tripped? Unplug that device.

2Check for moisture — Is the outlet wet? Is there standing water nearby? Address moisture first.

3Press the RESET button firmly — You should hear a click. The button should stay pressed in.

4Test with a different device — Plug in something you know works (like a phone charger). Does it have power?

5Test the original device elsewhere — If the circuit is fine but trips again with the same device, that device may be faulty.

Monthly GFCI Testing

GFCIs should be tested monthly to ensure they're working properly:

  1. Plug in a light or radio (something that will show it's on)
  2. Press the TEST button—the light should go off
  3. Press the RESET button—the light should come back on
  4. If either step fails, the GFCI needs to be replaced
Stop and Call a Pro If...
  • The GFCI won't reset at all (keeps popping out)
  • The GFCI trips immediately after resetting with nothing plugged in
  • You smell burning or see scorch marks
  • The outlet feels warm or hot
  • The GFCI fails the monthly test
  • You have no GFCI protection in required areas (install by electrician)

GFCI Breakers

Some homes have GFCI protection at the circuit breaker panel instead of (or in addition to) outlet-level GFCIs. These look like regular breakers but have a TEST button. They protect the entire circuit.

If you have no GFCI outlets but your bathroom still has protection, check your breaker panel for GFCI breakers.

GFCI vs. AFCI

  • GFCI (Ground Fault): Protects against electrical shock from current going through water or people. Required near water.
  • AFCI (Arc Fault): Protects against fires from electrical arcing (damaged wires, loose connections). Required in bedrooms and living areas in newer homes.

Some newer outlets are "dual function" (AFCI + GFCI).

Renter Note

Renters: If your bathroom, kitchen, or outdoor outlets lack GFCI protection, notify your landlord in writing. This is a significant safety issue. Modern electrical codes require them, and many landlords will upgrade on request.

Homeowners: If you have an older home without GFCIs in required areas, adding them is one of the most important electrical upgrades you can make. An electrician can typically install them for $100-200 per outlet.

Action Items

  1. Locate all GFCI outlets in your home
  2. Test each one using the TEST/RESET buttons
  3. Note any outlets near water that LACK GFCI protection
  4. Add "monthly GFCI test" to your maintenance calendar
