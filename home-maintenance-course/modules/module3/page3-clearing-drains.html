Page 3: Clearing Drains Safely

Slow or clogged drains are inevitable, but most clogs near the drain opening can be cleared without calling a plumber. The key is using the right method for the situation.

SAFETY WARNING: Chemical Drain Cleaners
  • NEVER mix different drain cleaning products (can create toxic gas)
  • NEVER use chemical cleaners in standing water
  • NEVER use in toilets (can crack porcelain)
  • Chemical cleaners can damage pipes with repeated use
  • If you've used chemicals and they didn't work, TELL THE PLUMBER

Method 1: The Plunger (Best First Try)

Two Types of Plungers:
  • Cup plunger: Flat bottom—for sinks, tubs, showers
  • Flange plunger: Has a soft rubber flap—for toilets

Using the wrong type won't work well!

For Sinks and Tubs:

1Block the overflow hole (tape or wet rag)—this creates suction

2Add enough water to cover the plunger cup

3Place plunger over drain, ensuring a seal

4Push down and pull up vigorously 15-20 times

5Pull plunger away quickly to break seal

6Test if water drains—repeat if needed

For Toilets:

  1. Don't flush again if water is high (it may overflow!)
  2. Insert flange plunger at an angle to fill with water
  3. Fit flange into drain hole, seal cup against porcelain
  4. Push and pull vigorously without breaking the seal
  5. Pull away to check—water should drain
  6. Flush to test

Method 2: Remove and Clean the P-Trap

For sink clogs that won't plunge, the clog may be in the curved P-trap underneath.

1Place a bucket under the P-trap to catch water

2Loosen slip nuts on both ends (usually hand-tight or need pliers)

3Remove P-trap—water will spill into bucket

4Clean out hair, gunk, and debris

5Reinstall and hand-tighten slip nuts

6Run water to test—check for leaks at connections

Method 3: Drain Snake (Drain Auger)

For clogs beyond the P-trap, a hand-crank drain snake ($15-30) can reach 15-25 feet into the pipe.

  1. Insert the snake into the drain opening
  2. Turn the crank clockwise while pushing forward
  3. When you hit resistance, continue cranking—the snake breaks through or grabs the clog
  4. Pull back slowly, keeping tension
  5. Clean the snake, repeat if needed
  6. Flush with hot water

Method 4: Baking Soda and Vinegar (Maintenance/Mild Clogs)

A gentler option that won't damage pipes:

  1. Pour 1/2 cup baking soda down the drain
  2. Follow with 1/2 cup white vinegar
  3. Cover the drain and wait 15-30 minutes
  4. Flush with hot (not boiling) water

This works best for maintenance and slow drains, not complete clogs.

Prevention Tips

  • Use drain covers to catch hair (especially in showers)
  • Never pour grease down the drain—wipe pans with paper towels first
  • Run hot water after each use to flush the drain
  • Monthly: baking soda + vinegar maintenance
  • Don't flush "flushable" wipes (they're not really flushable)
Stop and Call a Pro If...
  • Multiple drains are clogged at once (main line issue)
  • Water backs up in other fixtures when you drain one
  • You've tried all methods and nothing works
  • There's a sewage smell
  • Drains keep clogging repeatedly in the same spot

Action Items

  1. Check that you have the right type of plunger (or buy both types)
  2. Look under a sink and identify the P-trap
  3. If you have a slow drain, try the baking soda/vinegar method
