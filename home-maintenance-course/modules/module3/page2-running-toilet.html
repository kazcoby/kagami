Page 2: Fixing a Running Toilet

A running toilet can waste 200+ gallons of water per day—that's $100+ per month on your water bill! The good news: 90% of running toilets can be fixed in under 30 minutes with no special tools.

Cost Comparison:
  • Plumber visit for running toilet: $150-300
  • DIY replacement parts: $10-25
  • Your time: 15-30 minutes

Understanding Toilet Parts

Inside the Tank

  • Fill valve: The tall mechanism (usually left side) that refills the tank after flushing
  • Float: Ball or cup that rises with water level and tells fill valve when to stop
  • Flapper: Rubber seal at the bottom that lifts when you flush, then closes to hold water
  • Flush valve: The large opening the flapper covers
  • Overflow tube: Vertical tube that prevents tank from overflowing if fill valve fails
  • Handle/lever: Connects to flapper via chain

Diagnosing the Problem

The Dye Test

  1. Remove tank lid and set aside carefully (they break!)
  2. Add 5-10 drops of food coloring to the tank water
  3. Wait 15-20 minutes (don't flush!)
  4. Check the bowl—if you see colored water, the flapper is leaking

Common Causes and Fixes

Problem 1: Worn Flapper (Most Common)

Signs: Toilet runs intermittently or constantly; dye test shows leak.

1Turn off water supply (valve behind toilet—turn clockwise)

2Flush to empty the tank

3Unhook the old flapper from the flush valve ears and disconnect the chain

4Take the old flapper to the hardware store to match the size

5Install new flapper—hook onto ears, connect chain (leave slight slack)

6Turn water back on and test

Problem 2: Chain Too Short or Long

Signs: Handle sticks, flapper doesn't close fully, or flapper won't lift.

Fix: Adjust chain length. There should be about 1/2 inch of slack when the flapper is closed.

Problem 3: Water Level Too High

Signs: Water constantly flows into overflow tube.

Fix: Adjust the float:

  • Ball float: Bend the arm down slightly, or turn the screw on top
  • Cup float: Pinch the clip and slide the float down on the rod

Water level should be about 1 inch below the overflow tube.

Problem 4: Faulty Fill Valve

Signs: Water runs constantly, won't shut off even with float adjustment; you hear hissing.

Fix: Replace the entire fill valve ($10-20, 15-minute job). Follow package instructions.

Stop and Call a Pro If...
  • Water is leaking from the BASE of the toilet (wax ring issue)
  • The toilet rocks or moves when you sit on it
  • You see cracks in the porcelain
  • The problem persists after replacing flapper and fill valve
  • You're not comfortable working on it

Action Items

  1. Lift the lid on a toilet and identify the parts
  2. Perform the dye test on your toilet(s)
  3. Check that you know where the shutoff valve is
  4. If you have a running toilet, try the fixes above!
