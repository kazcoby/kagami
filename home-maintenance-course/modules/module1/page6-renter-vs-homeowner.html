Page 6: Renter vs. Homeowner Responsibilities

One of the most common questions in home maintenance is: "Is this my responsibility or my landlord's?" The answer depends on your lease, local laws, and the nature of the problem. This page clarifies the typical division of responsibilities.

Key Principles for Renters

1. Read Your Lease

Your lease may specify responsibilities differently than the general rules above. The lease is the governing document (as long as it doesn't violate local laws).

2. Document Everything

When you report a problem, do it in writing (email or text). Keep copies of all communications. Take photos of issues when they arise.

3. Report Problems Promptly

Waiting to report a leak that causes major damage could make you liable. Report issues as soon as you notice them.

4. Know Your Local Laws

Tenant rights vary significantly by state and city. Many areas have tenant advocacy organizations that can help you understand your rights.

When the Landlord Won't Respond

Emergency Situations

If you have a genuine emergency (no heat in winter, major water leak, gas smell) and can't reach your landlord:

  1. Document your attempts to contact them
  2. Address immediate safety concerns (evacuate if gas, shut off water if leak)
  3. Call the appropriate utility company
  4. In life-threatening situations, call 911
  5. Check your lease for emergency repair procedures

For Non-Emergencies

  1. Submit a written maintenance request (keep a copy)
  2. Follow up with a dated letter if no response
  3. Document all communication attempts
  4. Contact local tenant advocacy or housing authority if needed
  5. Know your local laws about "repair and deduct" or rent withholding

For Homeowners: It's All You!

If you own your home, you're responsible for everything. However:

  • Home warranty: May cover some repairs (read the fine print)
  • HOA: May handle exterior/common area maintenance
  • Insurance: Covers some damage (but not maintenance)
  • Warranties: New appliances and systems may still be under manufacturer warranty

Action Items

  1. If renting: Review your lease for maintenance responsibilities
  2. Save your landlord's/property manager's contact info (and after-hours emergency number)
  3. Know how to submit a maintenance request in writing
  4. If you own: Review your home warranty and insurance coverage
