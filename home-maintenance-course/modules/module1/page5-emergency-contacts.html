How to Find Trustworthy Professionals

Before You Need Them

  • Ask neighbors: "Who do you call for plumbing?" Personal referrals are gold.
  • Check reviews: Look for patterns in reviews, not just star ratings
  • Verify licensing: Most states have online databases to verify contractor licenses
  • Get recommendations from your real estate agent, home inspector, or insurance agent
  • Call for a small job first: See how they work before you have an emergency

Red Flags to Avoid

  • No physical address or only a P.O. box
  • Pressure to decide immediately
  • Cash-only payment requirements
  • Unwillingness to provide written estimates
  • No proof of insurance
  • "Just happened to be in the neighborhood"
  • Extremely low bids (often means corners will be cut)

What to Know Before You Call

When calling for service, be ready to provide:

  1. Your address (obviously, but have it ready)
  2. Description of the problem (what's happening, when it started)
  3. What you've already tried (shut off water, reset breaker, etc.)
  4. Brand/model of affected equipment (if applicable)
  5. Your availability (when can you be home?)
  6. Whether it's an emergency (true emergencies cost more)
Pro Tip: Save your emergency contacts in your phone with a prefix like "HOME-" so they're easy to find. Example: "HOME-Plumber" or "HOME-Electric Co"

Understanding Service Calls

Emergency vs. Standard Service

  • Emergency/After-hours: Typically 1.5x to 2x normal rates, but they'll come NOW
  • Same-day: May cost a premium, but less than emergency rates
  • Scheduled: Usually the best rates, book when you can wait

Common Fee Structures

  • Service call fee: $50-150 just to show up (often applied to repair cost)
  • Diagnostic fee: Fee to identify the problem
  • Hourly rate + parts: Common for smaller repairs
  • Flat rate: Set price for specific repairs (ask for this when possible)

Storing Your Contact List

  • Phone contacts: Add to your phone with the HOME- prefix
  • Refrigerator list: Old school, but works when your phone is dead
  • Home binder: Keep a physical folder with all home documents
  • Shared note: Share with household members (Google Keep, Apple Notes, etc.)
  • Near the panel: Tape utility numbers near your electrical panel

Action Items

  1. Find your utility company phone numbers (check your bills)
  2. Ask a neighbor or friend for contractor recommendations
  3. Add at least your utilities and landlord/insurance to your phone
