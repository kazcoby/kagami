Landlord & HOA Request Templates

Instructions: Copy and customize these templates for your maintenance requests. Always send requests in writing (email preferred) to create a documented record. Keep copies of all correspondence.

Template 1: Standard Maintenance Request

Use for: Routine repairs (leaky faucet, running toilet, broken appliance, etc.)

Subject: Maintenance Request - [Issue] at [Unit/Address]

Dear [Landlord/Property Manager Name],

I am writing to report a maintenance issue at [Your Full Address/Unit Number].

Issue Description:
[Describe the problem in detail. What is happening? Where exactly is the problem? When did you first notice it?]

Location in Unit: [Specific room/area]

When Noticed: [Date]

Urgency Level: [Routine / Urgent / Emergency]

What I've Tried: [Any troubleshooting steps you've already taken]

I have attached photos documenting the issue.

Please let me know when someone can address this. My availability for access is: [Days/times]

Best contact: [Phone number and/or email]

Thank you for your prompt attention to this matter.

Sincerely,
[Your Name]
[Unit/Address]
[Phone Number]

Template 2: Urgent/Emergency Request

Use for: No heat in winter, no hot water, flooding, major leak, broken lock, electrical hazard

Subject: URGENT Maintenance Request - [Issue] at [Unit/Address]

Dear [Landlord/Property Manager Name],

This is an urgent maintenance request requiring immediate attention.

Emergency Issue: [Describe the problem]

Location: [Address/Unit]

Current Status: [Is the situation getting worse? Any immediate safety concerns?]

Actions Taken: [Any emergency steps you've taken, such as shutting off water]

This issue requires attention within [24 hours / same day / immediately] due to [reason - safety hazard, habitability, property damage, etc.].

Please contact me immediately at [Phone Number].

I am available for access: [Anytime today / specific times]

[Your Name]
[Unit/Address]

For True Emergencies: Call 911 for fire, gas leak, or immediate danger. Call your landlord's emergency line (if they have one) AND send the written request as documentation.

Template 3: Follow-Up Request (No Response)

Use for: When you haven't received a response to your original request

Subject: Follow-Up: Maintenance Request from [Original Date] - [Issue]

Dear [Landlord/Property Manager Name],

I am following up on my maintenance request submitted on [date of original request] regarding [brief description of issue] at [Unit/Address].

I have not yet received a response or had the issue addressed. The original request is attached/quoted below for reference.

Current Status: [Has the problem gotten worse? Any new developments?]

Days Since Original Request: [Number]

I would appreciate an update on when this repair can be scheduled. Please contact me at [Phone/Email].

Thank you for your attention to this matter.

Sincerely,
[Your Name]

Original Request:
[Copy and paste your original request here]

Template 4: Request for GFCI/Safety Device Installation

Use for: Missing safety devices (GFCIs, smoke detectors, CO detectors)

Subject: Request for Safety Device Installation - [Unit/Address]

Dear [Landlord/Property Manager Name],

I am writing to request the installation of [GFCI outlets / smoke detectors / CO detectors] at [Unit/Address].

Current Situation:
[Describe what's missing. For example: "The bathroom outlets are standard outlets without GFCI protection" or "There is no smoke detector in the bedroom."]

Relevant Code Requirements:
Current building and electrical codes require [GFCI protection in bathrooms and kitchens / smoke detectors in bedrooms and hallways / CO detectors near sleeping areas in homes with gas appliances].

These safety devices protect against [electrical shock / fire / carbon monoxide poisoning] and their installation is typically a landlord responsibility under local housing codes.

Please let me know when these can be installed. I am available [your availability].

Thank you for your attention to this safety matter.

Sincerely,
[Your Name]
[Unit/Address]

Tips for Effective Requests

  • Always put it in writing — Email creates automatic timestamps
  • Be specific — Include exact location, what's happening, when it started
  • Include photos/videos — Document the problem before repair
  • Note urgency level — Emergency, urgent, or routine
  • Provide availability — Makes scheduling easier
  • Keep copies — Save all correspondence
  • Follow up — If no response in reasonable time, follow up in writing
  • Know your rights — Look up local tenant rights for required response times
If Landlord Doesn't Respond:
  1. Follow up in writing (use Template 3)
  2. Send via certified mail (creates proof of receipt)
  3. Document everything with dates and photos
  4. Know your local tenant rights and required response times
  5. Contact local housing authority for habitability issues
  6. Consider tenant legal aid for persistent problems
