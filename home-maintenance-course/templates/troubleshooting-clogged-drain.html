TROUBLESHOOTING: Clogged Drain

One-Page Quick Reference Guide

⚠️ CHEMICAL DRAIN CLEANER SAFETY:
  • NEVER mix different drain products (toxic gas!)
  • NEVER use in standing water
  • NEVER use in toilets (can crack porcelain)
  • If chemicals were used, TELL THE PLUMBER

Try Methods in This Order

Method 1: Plunger (Try First!)

For Sinks/Tubs:

  1. Block overflow hole (tape or wet rag)
  2. Add water to cover plunger cup
  3. Place plunger, ensure seal
  4. Push/pull vigorously 15-20 times
  5. Pull away quickly
  6. Test drain, repeat if needed

For Toilets:

  1. DON'T flush if water is high!
  2. Use FLANGE plunger (has rubber flap)
  3. Insert at angle to fill with water
  4. Fit flange into drain, seal cup
  5. Plunge without breaking seal
  6. Pull away to check
Plunger Type Use For
Cup (flat bottom) Sinks, tubs, showers
Flange (rubber flap) Toilets

Method 2: Clean the P-Trap (Sink Clogs)

1Place bucket under P-trap

2Loosen slip nuts (hand-tight or use pliers)

3Remove P-trap, let water fall in bucket

4Clean out debris, hair, gunk

5Reinstall, hand-tighten slip nuts

6Run water, check for leaks

Method 3: Drain Snake ($15-30)

1Insert snake into drain

2Turn crank clockwise while pushing

3When you hit resistance, keep cranking

4Pull back slowly with tension

5Clean snake, repeat if needed

6Flush with hot water

Method 4: Baking Soda + Vinegar (Mild/Maintenance)

1Pour 1/2 cup baking soda down drain

2Pour 1/2 cup white vinegar

3Cover drain, wait 15-30 minutes

4Flush with hot (not boiling) water

Best for: slow drains and monthly maintenance, NOT complete clogs

STOP and Call a Pro If:
  • Multiple drains clogged at once (main line issue)
  • Water backs up in other fixtures
  • Sewage smell
  • Drains keep clogging in same spot
  • You've tried all methods with no success
Prevention:
  • Use drain covers to catch hair
  • Never pour grease down drain
  • Run hot water after each use
  • Monthly: baking soda + vinegar
  • Don't flush "flushable" wipes
