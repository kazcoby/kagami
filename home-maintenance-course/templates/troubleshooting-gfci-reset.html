TROUBLESHOOTING: GFCI Reset

One-Page Quick Reference Guide

What is a GFCI? Ground Fault Circuit Interrupter — a safety device that cuts power in 1/40th of a second if electricity goes somewhere dangerous (like through water or a person).

Identifying GFCI Outlets

GFCI outlets have two buttons:

[TEST]   [RESET]

TEST button (usually black/darker) | RESET button (usually red, may pop out)

Found in: bathrooms, kitchens, garages, outdoors, basements, laundry areas

How to Reset a Tripped GFCI

1Identify what was plugged in — Unplug that device

2Check for moisture — Is outlet or device wet? Address first.

3Press RESET button firmly — Should click and stay in

4Test with different device — Plug in something you know works

5Test original device elsewhere — If GFCI trips again with same device, device may be faulty

Why GFCIs Trip

Common Causes:

  • Moisture in outlet/device
  • Worn appliances (old hair dryers, etc.)
  • Overloaded circuit
  • Worn/old GFCI (10-15 year lifespan)
  • Ground fault in wiring

Common Culprits:

  • Hair dryers / curling irons
  • Space heaters
  • Power tools
  • Appliances with frayed cords
  • Devices used near water

Monthly GFCI Test (Do This!)

  1. Plug in a lamp or radio
  2. Press TEST button — device should turn OFF
  3. Press RESET button — device should turn back ON
  4. If either step fails → GFCI needs replacement
One GFCI Can Protect Many Outlets!

If a regular outlet has no power, check for tripped GFCIs nearby. A bathroom GFCI might protect outlets in other bathrooms or even the garage. Check ALL GFCIs before assuming a circuit problem.

GFCI Won't Reset? Troubleshooting

  • Button won't stay in: Unplug ALL devices from that circuit, try again
  • Still won't reset: Check circuit breaker (may be tripped too)
  • Reset but no power: GFCI may be worn out and need replacement
  • Check other GFCIs: One upstream GFCI may control this outlet
STOP and Call an Electrician If:
  • GFCI won't reset at all (keeps popping out)
  • GFCI trips immediately with nothing plugged in
  • Burning smell or scorch marks
  • Outlet feels warm or hot
  • GFCI fails monthly test
  • No GFCI protection in required areas (needs installation)
Where GFCIs Are Required:
  • All bathroom outlets
  • Kitchen outlets near sink
  • Garage outlets
  • Outdoor outlets
  • Basements and crawl spaces
  • Laundry areas
  • Near pools, hot tubs, fountains

Renters: If your home lacks GFCIs in these areas, notify landlord in writing.
