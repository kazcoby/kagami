Text Alternative: DIY vs. Pro Reference Guide

The decision flowchart above asks these questions in order:

  1. Is this an emergency? (gas leak, flooding, fire, dangerous cold) → If YES, call 911 or emergency services immediately
  2. Does it involve gas, electrical wiring, or structural elements? → If YES, call a licensed professional
  3. Do you have the tools and skills? → If NO, call a pro or learn first
  4. Could a mistake cause major damage or be dangerous? → If YES, call a pro
  5. If you answered NO to all danger questions and YES to having skills → DIY is appropriate

Key Questions Before DIYing

Safe to DIY if you can answer YES to ALL:

  • Is this task covered in basic home maintenance guides?
  • Do I have (or can I get) the right tools?
  • Am I comfortable with the safety requirements?
  • If I mess up, is it easily reversible?
  • Does this NOT involve gas, high-voltage electricity, or structure?

Call a Pro if ANY of these apply:

  • The task involves gas lines
  • The task involves the electrical panel or new wiring
  • The task affects structural elements
  • Permits are required
  • A mistake could cause injury or major property damage
  • The problem is getting worse despite your efforts
  • You're uncomfortable or uncertain
