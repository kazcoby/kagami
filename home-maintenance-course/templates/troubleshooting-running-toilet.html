TROUBLESHOOTING: Running Toilet

One-Page Quick Reference Guide

Why Fix It? A running toilet can waste 200+ gallons/day = $100+/month on your water bill!

Step 1: Diagnose the Problem

The Dye Test

  1. Remove tank lid (set aside carefully!)
  2. Add 5-10 drops of food coloring to tank water
  3. Wait 15-20 minutes (don't flush!)
  4. Check bowl: Color in bowl = leaking flapper

Step 2: Identify the Cause

PROBLEM: Worn Flapper

Signs: Toilet runs intermittently; dye test positive

Fix: Replace flapper ($5-10)

PROBLEM: Chain Issues

Signs: Handle sticks; flapper doesn't close fully

Fix: Adjust chain (1/2" slack when closed)

PROBLEM: Water Level Too High

Signs: Water flows into overflow tube

Fix: Lower the float (bend arm or adjust clip)

PROBLEM: Bad Fill Valve

Signs: Constant running; hissing sound

Fix: Replace fill valve ($10-20)

Adjusting Water Level

  • Ball float: Bend arm down or turn screw on top
  • Cup float: Pinch clip, slide float down on rod
  • Target: Water 1 inch below overflow tube
STOP and Call a Pro If:
  • Water leaking from BASE of toilet (wax ring issue)
  • Toilet rocks or moves when you sit
  • Cracks visible in porcelain
  • Problem persists after trying these fixes
Supplies to Keep on Hand:
  • Universal flapper ($5-10)
  • Food coloring (for dye test)
  • Adjustable wrench (for stubborn shutoff valves)
Cost Comparison: DIY fix: $5-20 | Plumber visit: $150-300
