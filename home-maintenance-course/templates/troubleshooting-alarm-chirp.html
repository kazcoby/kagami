TROUBLESHOOTING: Alarm Chirping

One-Page Quick Reference Guide

⚠️ FIRST: Is This an Emergency?
  • Continuous loud alarm = possible real emergency. Evacuate first, then investigate!
  • CO detector alarming = Get everyone out, call 911, don't re-enter until cleared
  • Single chirp every 30-60 seconds = Low battery (not emergency, but fix soon)

What the Sounds Mean

Sound Pattern Meaning Action
Continuous loud alarm Smoke or CO detected Evacuate! Call 911 if real
Single chirp every 30-60 sec Low battery Replace battery
Chirping after battery change Needs reset or end of life Reset or replace unit
3 beeps, pause, 3 beeps Smoke alarm pattern Check for smoke/fire
4 beeps, pause, 4 beeps CO alarm pattern Evacuate immediately!
5 beeps/chirps End of life (10-year limit) Replace entire unit

How to Stop the Chirping

Step 1: Replace the Battery

1Locate the chirping unit (follow the sound!)

2Remove from ceiling/wall (twist counterclockwise, pull down, or remove screws)

3Open battery compartment

4Replace with FRESH battery (correct type—usually 9V or AA)

5Reinstall unit, press TEST button

Step 2: If Still Chirping After Battery Change

1Remove the battery

2Press and hold TEST button for 15-20 seconds (drains residual charge)

3Reinstall battery

4Test again

Hardwired units: Also trip the breaker for 30 seconds

Still Chirping? The unit may be at end of life. Check the manufacture date on the back:
  • Smoke detectors: Replace every 10 years
  • CO detectors: Replace every 5-7 years
  • Combo units: Replace every 5-7 years (CO sensor expires first)

Quick Reference: Battery Types

Most Common:

  • 9-volt (rectangular)
  • AA (1 or 2)
  • AAA (rare)

Pro Tip:

  • Use quality alkaline batteries
  • Don't use rechargeable
  • Replace ALL detector batteries at same time (twice yearly)

Can't Reach It?

  • Use a sturdy step stool or ladder (never a chair!)
  • Some have a "hush" button you can press with a broom handle
  • Renters: Contact landlord/maintenance if you can't safely reach
  • Never remove batteries and leave them out—it's dangerous!
Prevention: Change Batteries Twice a Year

Many people change batteries when clocks change for daylight saving time (March & November). Put it on your calendar!

Renters: If smoke/CO detectors are missing, non-functional, or at end of life, notify your landlord in writing. Working detectors are typically a legal requirement for landlords.
