Answer Key with Explanations

(For instructor use — not shown to students until after submission)

Question 1: B. Replace or clean the air filter regularly

While professional maintenance is important, filter replacement is the single most impactful thing you can do. A clogged filter restricts airflow, causes the system to work harder, and is the #1 cause of HVAC system failures. It's also something anyone can do without professional help.

Question 2: B. 5-15%

According to the Department of Energy, a dirty filter can increase energy consumption by 5-15%. The system has to work harder to push air through a clogged filter, using more electricity and putting strain on components.

Question 3: B. Toward the furnace/air handler

The arrow indicates airflow direction. Air flows FROM the room, THROUGH the filter, and TO the furnace/air handler. Installing the filter backwards reduces its effectiveness and can cause damage.

Question 4: B. 35-38°F

The FDA recommends keeping your refrigerator at 40°F or below, but 35-38°F is the sweet spot. Colder risks freezing items; warmer risks food safety. A refrigerator thermometer is the most accurate way to verify your temperature.

Question 5: D. Both A and B

Lint buildup is a serious fire hazard (causing over 15,000 home fires annually) AND reduces dryer efficiency, making clothes take longer to dry. The lint trap should be cleaned after every load, and the vent should be professionally cleaned annually.

Question 6: B. 120°F

120°F is the recommended setting for both safety and efficiency. Many water heaters come set to 140°F, which wastes energy and increases scalding risk. Lowering from 140°F to 120°F can save $36-61 per year.

Question 7: B. 1-3%

The Department of Energy estimates 1-3% savings per degree of adjustment when maintained for 8+ hours. This is why programmable thermostats are so effective—small adjustments during sleep or away time add up to significant annual savings.
