Assignment: Plumbing Inspection Checklist

Module: 3 - Water + Moisture

Points: 25

Due: End of Week 3

Submission: PDF, Word document, or typed directly into Canvas

Overview

This week, you'll conduct a thorough plumbing and moisture inspection of your home. This hands-on activity applies everything you've learned about water systems, leak detection, and moisture control.

Instructions

Part 1: Toilet Inspection (8 points)

For each toilet in your home (up to 3), complete the following:

  1. Location: Where is this toilet located? (e.g., "master bathroom," "half bath downstairs")
  2. Dye Test: Perform the dye test described in Page 2. Did any color appear in the bowl after 15-20 minutes?
  3. Visual Check: Open the tank lid and identify:
    • Fill valve (describe location)
    • Flapper (does it look worn or warped?)
    • Chain (is there appropriate slack?)
    • Water level (about 1 inch below overflow tube?)
  4. Floor Check: Feel the floor around the toilet base. Any soft spots, dampness, or staining?
  5. Supply Valve: Locate the shutoff valve. Can you turn it? Does it look corroded?

If you have more than 3 toilets, inspect your 3 most-used ones.

Part 2: Under-Sink Inspection (8 points)

Inspect under at least 3 sinks (combination of bathroom and kitchen):

  1. Location: Which sink? (e.g., "kitchen," "upstairs bathroom")
  2. P-Trap: Can you identify the P-trap? Describe its condition (PVC, metal, any corrosion?)
  3. Supply Lines: Describe the supply line connections. Any visible drips, water stains, or mineral deposits?
  4. Cabinet Floor: Feel the cabinet floor. Is it dry? Any warping, staining, or water damage?
  5. Garbage Disposal (kitchen only): Check connections around the disposal if you have one

Part 3: GFCI Test (4 points)

  1. Locate all GFCI outlets in your home (check bathrooms, kitchen, garage, outdoors)
  2. List how many you found and their locations
  3. Test at least 2 GFCIs using the TEST/RESET buttons. Did they work properly?
  4. Note any areas near water that do NOT have GFCI protection

Part 4: Moisture/Humidity Assessment (5 points)

  1. Humidity Level: If you have a hygrometer or smart thermostat with humidity, what is your current indoor humidity level?
  2. Ventilation Check: Do your bathrooms have exhaust fans? Do they work? Where do they vent?
  3. Signs of Moisture Issues: Look for:
    • Condensation on windows
    • Musty smells
    • Visible mold or mildew anywhere
    • Peeling paint or wallpaper
  4. High-Risk Areas: Check your basement (if applicable), under water heater, and behind washing machine for signs of moisture

Submission Format

Organize your submission with clear headings for each part. Include:

  • Your name and date
  • Clear answers for each inspection item
  • Any photos that help document your findings (optional but encouraged)
  • A brief summary (2-3 sentences) of anything you discovered that needs attention
Tips for Success
  • Use your phone's flashlight to see under sinks clearly
  • Take photos as you go—they help with documentation and you can include them in your submission
  • If you live in an apartment with limited access, do what you can and note any limitations
  • If you find an issue, don't panic! Note it and determine if it's DIY or needs a pro
Safety Reminder

This is an inspection only—you're looking and documenting, not fixing anything yet. If you find issues:

  • Renters: Document and report to your landlord
  • Homeowners: Determine if it's a DIY fix (covered in this module) or needs a professional

After Completing This Assignment

You should now have a clear picture of your home's plumbing and moisture status. Keep this inspection as a baseline and consider:

  • Adding quarterly plumbing checks to your maintenance calendar
  • Addressing any issues you discovered
  • Purchasing any tools you identified as helpful (hygrometer, leak detectors, etc.)
