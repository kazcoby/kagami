Assignment: Create Your Personal Maintenance Calendar

Module: 4 - Simple Repairs + Maintenance System

Points: 30

Due: End of Week 4

Submission: PDF, Word document, screenshot of digital calendar, or typed directly into Canvas

Overview

This capstone assignment brings together everything you've learned in the course. You'll create a personalized maintenance calendar for YOUR specific home, accounting for whether you rent or own, your climate, and your home's unique features.

Instructions

Part 1: Home Profile (5 points)

Create a brief profile of your home:

  1. Housing type: Apartment, condo, townhouse, single-family home?
  2. Renter or homeowner?
  3. Approximate age of home: (estimate if unsure)
  4. Climate: Hot/cold extremes? High humidity? Freeze risk?
  5. Heating/cooling type: Central HVAC, window units, baseboard, etc.?
  6. Special features: Fireplace? Pool? Septic? Well water? Older appliances?

This profile helps explain why your calendar includes (or excludes) certain tasks.

Part 2: Monthly Task List (8 points)

Create your personalized monthly checklist. Include:

  • At least 5 monthly tasks appropriate for YOUR home
  • Brief notes on each task (what specifically you'll check/do)
  • Indicate any tasks that are N/A and explain why

Example format:

Task My Notes
Check HVAC filter Filter location: hallway closet. Current filter size: 20x25x1. Replace every 30 days during summer (high pollen).
Test smoke detectors 3 detectors: hallway, bedroom, kitchen. Press test button on each.

Part 3: Seasonal Calendar (10 points)

Create a year-at-a-glance calendar showing seasonal tasks. This can be:

  • A table with columns for Spring, Summer, Fall, Winter
  • A screenshot of recurring calendar events you've set up
  • A filled-out version of our course template

Requirements:

  • At least 3-5 tasks per season
  • Tasks must be appropriate for YOUR specific situation (renter vs. owner, climate)
  • Include at least 2 annual professional services if you're a homeowner
  • Note any tasks you'll skip and why (e.g., "N/A - no fireplace")

Part 4: Emergency Contact List (4 points)

Create or screenshot your emergency contact list including:

  • At least 3 professional contacts (plumber, electrician, HVAC, handyman, etc.)
  • For renters: Landlord/property manager contact info, maintenance request procedure
  • Utility company emergency numbers
  • Note how you found/verified these contacts

If you haven't found all contacts yet, list the services you need to research and your plan for finding them.

Part 5: Reflection (3 points)

Write a brief reflection (100-150 words) addressing:

  • What's one thing about home maintenance you didn't know before this course?
  • What's the most important maintenance task for YOUR specific home?
  • How confident do you feel about tackling basic home maintenance now compared to before the course?

Submission Format

Your submission should include:

  • Your name and date
  • Clearly labeled sections for each part
  • Tables, lists, or calendar screenshots as appropriate
  • The reflection paragraph at the end
Tips for Success
  • Reference the seasonal task lists in Page 5 as a starting point
  • Customize! Don't just copy the course listsâ€”tailor them to YOUR home
  • If you're a renter, focus on tasks that are YOUR responsibility
  • Consider your climate (no winterizing for Miami, no A/C prep for Minnesota winters)
  • Screenshots of digital calendars are perfectly acceptable
  • The more specific your notes, the more useful your calendar will be
Common Mistakes to Avoid
  • Copying the course lists without customization
  • Including tasks that don't apply (e.g., gutter cleaning for apartment renters)
  • Forgetting to explain WHY certain tasks don't apply
  • Missing the reflection section
