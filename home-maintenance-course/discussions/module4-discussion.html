Discussion: Your Maintenance Wins

Module: 4 - Simple Repairs + Maintenance System

Points: 10

Due: End of Week 4

Discussion Prompt

Congratulations! You've made it through all four modules of the course. This final discussion is a celebration of what you've learned and a chance to share your experiences with your classmates.

Part 1: Your DIY Victory (or Lesson Learned)

Share a home maintenance or repair task you attempted during this course (or recently). This could be:

  • A squeaky hinge you silenced
  • A toilet you diagnosed or fixed
  • Caulk you replaced
  • A filter you changed
  • A leak you found and addressed
  • A GFCI you tested and reset
  • A wall hole you patched
  • Or ANY home maintenance task!

Tell us:

  1. What was the task?
  2. How did it go? (Success, partial success, or learning experience)
  3. What would you do differently next time?
  4. Would you tackle a similar task again? Why or why not?

Part 2: Your Maintenance Plan

Share your plan for staying on top of home maintenance going forward:

  • What system will you use? (Calendar, app, paper checklist?)
  • Which monthly tasks are most important for YOUR home?
  • Is there anything you learned that you'll definitely put into practice?

Part 3: Pay It Forward

Share ONE tip you'd give to someone just starting to learn about home maintenance. What's the most valuable thing you learned that you wish you'd known sooner?

Response Requirements

Initial Post (Due by Thursday)

  • Minimum 200 words
  • Address all three parts
  • Be specific about your experiences and plans
  • Photos welcome (but not required)

Peer Responses (Due by Sunday)

  • Reply to at least 2 classmates
  • Minimum 50 words each
  • Celebrate their wins, offer encouragement, share related tips
  • Ask follow-up questions about their experiences

Grading Rubric

Criteria Points
Initial post addresses all three parts with specific details 4
Shares concrete maintenance plan for going forward 2
Two meaningful peer responses provided 3
Posted on time 1
Total 10
Discussion Tips
  • No "win" to share? That's okay! Share what you learned from your inspection activities or what you're planning to try.
  • Photos really help bring your story to life—before/after shots are great!
  • Be honest about challenges. Others learn from hearing what didn't work too.
  • This is a supportive community—celebrate your classmates' progress!
  • Your tip for Part 3 might be exactly what a classmate needs to hear.
