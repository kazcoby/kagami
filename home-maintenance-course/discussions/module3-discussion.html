Discussion: Water Wins and Woes

Module: 3 - Water + Moisture

Points: 10

Due: End of Week 3

Discussion Prompt

Water-related issues are some of the most common (and potentially expensive) home problems. This week, we're sharing our experiences and learning from each other.

Part 1: Share Your Story

Think about a water or moisture-related issue you've experienced in a home (your current home, a previous home, or even a family member's or friend's home). This could be:

  • A running toilet
  • A clogged drain
  • A leaky faucet or pipe
  • Water damage from a leak
  • Mold or humidity problems
  • A GFCI that kept tripping
  • A water heater issue
  • Or any other water-related problem!

Tell us:

  1. What was the problem?
  2. How did you (or someone else) discover it?
  3. Was it a DIY fix or did it require a professional?
  4. What was the outcome?
  5. Looking back, is there anything that could have prevented it or caught it earlier?

Part 2: Apply What You've Learned

Based on what you learned in this module, share one new piece of knowledge you'll apply to prevent water problems in your current home. This could be:

  • A monthly inspection habit you'll start
  • A tool or supply you'll purchase (like a hygrometer or leak detector)
  • A habit you'll change (like running exhaust fans longer)
  • Something you need to report to your landlord

Response Requirements

Initial Post (Due by Thursday)

  • Minimum 150 words
  • Address both Part 1 and Part 2
  • Be specific about the situation and your takeaways

Peer Responses (Due by Sunday)

  • Reply to at least 2 classmates
  • Minimum 50 words each
  • Offer helpful suggestions, ask follow-up questions, or share related experiences
  • Be supportive and constructive

Grading Rubric

Criteria Points
Initial post addresses both parts thoroughly 4
Story includes specific details and reflection 2
Two meaningful peer responses provided 3
Posted on time 1
Total 10
Discussion Tips
  • No water story? That's actually great news! Share what you found during your inspections this week instead.
  • Don't have experience with these issues? Interview a family member, friend, or neighbor about their experiences.
  • Photos are welcome (but not required) if they help illustrate your story!
  • Remember: We're all learning together. There are no "dumb" questions or experiences!
